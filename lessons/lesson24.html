Invitations & Suggestions

Level: A1-A2
Length: 60 min
Topic: Making, accepting & refusing invitations
Score: 0%

Lesson Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Use Let's, Why don't we..., How about..., Shall we... naturally
  • Accept: Good idea, I'd love to, Sure - Refuse politely: Sorry, I can't + reason
  • Pronounce rising intonation in suggestions & invitations
  • Offer alternatives: Maybe we could..., What if we...?
  • Avoid blunt refusals; maintain social harmony

Input & Explanation

1. Core Structures

Let's + base verb

(very common, friendly)

Let's go bowling.

Let's not stay late.

Why don't we/you + base...?

Why don't we order pizza?

How about/What about + noun/-ing?

How about a coffee?

What about going to the beach?

Shall we/I + base...?

(UK polite offer/suggestion)

Shall we start?

Shall I carry that?

2. Accepting (Positive Responses)

Good idea! / Great! / I'd love to. / Sure, let's do that. / Sounds good.
Add confirmation: OK, let's meet at seven.

3. Refusing (Negative + Reason + Alternative)

Sorry, I can't. I have to study.
I'd love to, but I'm busy tonight. Maybe another time?
That sounds fun, but I'm really tired. How about tomorrow instead?

NO blunt "No" alone – sounds rude.

4. Time & Place Phrases

tonight, after class, at the weekend, tomorrow afternoon, on Saturday, in the park, at your place, after work

5. Common Errors

❌ Wrong ✅ Correct
Let's to go out. Let's go out.
How about go shopping? How about going shopping?
Why don't go we to the cinema? Why don't we go to the cinema?
Shall we to watch a film? Shall we watch a film?
No, I can't coming. Sorry, I can't come.

Guided Practice

1. Choose correct form:
  1. Let's (watch) a film.
  2. How about (go) for a walk?
2. Accept positively:

go / concert / Saturday →

3. Refuse politely:

can't / homework / suggest tomorrow →

4. Transform:

We could order sushi. → start with How about... →

Individual Exercises

1. Form correct suggestion:
  1. Let's / eat / out →
  2. Why don't we / play / board games →
  3. How about / a coffee / after class →
  4. Shall we / start / now →
  5. What about / go / beach →
2. Accept invitations (positive reply):
  1. Let's watch a film tonight. →
  2. Shall we go shopping tomorrow? →
  3. How about a picnic on Sunday? →
3. Refuse with reason + alternative:
  1. Let's go jogging now. (tired / later) →
  2. Why don't we order pizza? (on diet / salad) →
  3. Shall we play video games? (exam tomorrow / after) → Sorry, I have an exam tomorrow. Maybe after the exam
4. Multiple choice – natural reply:

"Let's grab a coffee."

Good idea!
No.
I can grab.
