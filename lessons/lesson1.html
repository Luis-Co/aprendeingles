Lesson 1: English Articles (a, an, the)

Level: A1
Length: 60 min
Topic: Indefinite & definite articles
Score: 0%

Lesson Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Know when to use a, an and the
  • Tell the difference between definite and indefinite reference
  • Use articles correctly in short spoken and written sentences
  • Avoid common mistakes with countable nouns

Part A: Explanation & Examples

1. What is an article?

Articles are little words that come before nouns. They tell us if the noun is specific or not specific.

Type Article Meaning Example
Indefinite a / an Any one thing I saw a dog.
Definite the That exact thing The dog was white.

2. Indefinite articles: a vs an

  • a → before a consonant sound
    a book, a teacher, a university (/j/ sound = consonant)
  • an → before a vowel sound
    an apple, an hour, an honest man (silent h)

Rule: It's the sound, not the spelling.

3. Definite article: the

Use the when both speaker and listener know which thing is meant.

  • The sun is bright. (only one sun)
  • The book on the table is mine. (we can see it)
  • The students in this class are great. (specific group)

4. Zero article (∅)

No article is needed for:

  • general plurals: Cats are cute. ❌ The cats are cute. (only if specific)
  • uncountable nouns in general: I love music. ❌ I love the music. (unless specific)

5. Common mistakes

❌ Wrong ✅ Correct
She is a engineer. She is an engineer.
I need a information. I need some information.
The love is important. Love is important.

Part B: Interactive Practice

1. Fill in the blanks

1. I ate orange.
2. orange was sweet.
3. He wants umbrella.
4. milk in the fridge is old.
5. They are doctors.

2. Choose a or an

1. university
2. hour
3. European city
4. honest boy
5. apple

3. Multiple Choice

1. I have (a / an / the) idea.
a) a
b) an
c) the
2. (A / The) idea is interesting.
a) A
b) The
3. She is (a / an) artist.
a) an
b) a
4. I love (the / –) chocolate.
a) the
b) –
5. We watched (a / the) film yesterday. (specific)
a) a
b) the

4. Error Correction

1. He is a actor. →
2. I like the dogs. (speaking generally) →
3. She bought a bread. →

Part E: Self-Study Roadmap

Grammar Focus

  • Practice a/an with different nouns
  • Learn common exceptions (university, hour)
  • Study when to use "the" vs no article
  • Master countable vs uncountable nouns

Vocabulary

  • Learn 20 nouns that start with vowel sounds
  • Learn 20 nouns that start with consonant sounds
  • Practice common uncountable nouns

Skills Practice

  • Reading: identify articles in short texts
  • Listening: notice article usage in conversations
  • Speaking: describe objects using correct articles
  • Writing: write 5 sentences daily using articles
