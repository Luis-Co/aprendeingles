Lesson 7: Regular & Irregular Verbs

Level: A1-A2
Length: 60 min
Topic: Present vs. past forms, spelling rules
Score: 0%

Lesson Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Add -ed correctly to regular verbs (play→played, stop→stopped)
  • Recognize and memorize the three columns of 30 high-frequency irregulars
  • Pronounce -ed endings /t/ /d/ /ɪd/
  • Choose the right past form in simple sentences & questions

Part A: Explanation & Examples

1. Regular verbs – forming the past simple

Rule: base + -ed
work → worked /wɜːrkt/
clean → cleaned /d/

Spelling traps

  • verbs ending -e → +d
    live → lived
  • consonant + -y → -ied
    study → studied
  • short verbs, 1 vowel + 1 consonant → double
    stop → stopped, plan → planned

-ed pronunciation (phonetic):

/t/

after voiceless sounds

look→looked /lʊkt/

/d/

after voiced sounds

play→played /pleɪd/

/ɪd/

after t or d

want→wanted /ˈwɒntɪd/

2. Irregular verbs – no rule, must memorize

Three-column list (base – past – past participle)

Base Past Example
go went I went to Paris.
eat ate She ate pizza.
have had We had fun.
see saw They saw a film.
buy bought He bought a coat.
write wrote I wrote an e-mail.

Top 30 irregulars cover ≈ 80% of daily stories.

3. Negatives & questions (quick recap)

Regular & irregular share auxiliaries:
(+) I arrived. / I saw.
(–) I didn't arrive. / I didn't see. (base after didn't)
(?) Did you arrive? / Did you see? (base again)

Common learner slip: ✖️I didn't saw. → ✅I didn't see.

4. Signal words for past

yesterday, last night/week, two days ago, in 2020, when I was 10…

5. Pronunciation drills

Repeat:
/t/ helped, laughed, watched
/d/ played, cleaned, loved
/ɪd/ started, needed, visited

6. Common errors

❌ Wrong ✅ Correct
I goed home. I went home.
She didn't found it. She didn't find it.
We arived late. We arrived (double r)
He played good. He played well (adverb)

Part E: Self-Study Roadmap

Grammar Focus

  • Practice forming regular past tense verbs
  • Memorize common irregular verbs
  • Learn the pronunciation rules for -ed endings
  • Practice forming negatives and questions

Vocabulary

  • Learn the top 30 irregular verbs
  • Study past time expressions
  • Practice verb forms in different contexts

Skills Practice

  • Reading: identify past tense verbs in texts
  • Listening: notice past tense usage in conversations
  • Speaking: practice telling stories using past tense
  • Writing: write about past events using correct verb forms
