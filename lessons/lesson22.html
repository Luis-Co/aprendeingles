Comparative & Superlative Adjectives

Level: A1-A2
Length: 60 min
Topic: Short & long adjectives, spelling rules
Score: 0%

Lesson Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Form regular comparatives (-er, more) & superlatives (-est, most) correctly
  • Use than after comparative & the before superlative
  • Choose between -er/-est vs more/most by syllable count & spelling
  • Apply 4 irregular forms: good/better/best, bad/worse/worst, far/farther/farthest, little/less/least
  • Avoid classic errors: more better, the most cheapest

Input & Explanation

1. One-syllable adjectives → -er / -est

Adjective Comparative Superlative Example
tall taller the tallest Mount Everest is taller than Mont Blanc. It's the tallest mountain here.
cheap cheaper the cheapest This phone is cheaper.

Spelling rules for one-syllable adjectives

Double consonant

Consonant after short vowel → double

big → bigger → the biggest

hot → hotter → the hottest

-e ending

-e → -r / -st

large → larger → the largest

Consonant + y

consonant + y → -ier / -iest

busy → busier → the busiest

-ow ending

-ow → -er / -est (OK)

narrow → narrower → the narrowest

2. Two-syllable adjectives ending in -y, -le, -er, -ow → usually -er / -est

happy → happier → the happiest
simple → simpler → the simplest
clever → cleverer → the cleverest
Exception: boring → more boring (long vowel + ing)

3. Two+ syllables (not rule 2) → more / most

Adjective Comparative Superlative
modern more modern the most modern
expensive more expensive the most expensive
comfortable more comfortable the most comfortable

NO double forms: ✖️ more better → ✅ better

4. Irregular adjectives (must memorize)

Adjective Comparative Superlative
good better the best
bad worse the worst
far farther / further the farthest / the furthest
little (amount) less the least

5. Structures & prepositions

comparative + than
Paris is bigger than Rome.
the + superlative + in + place / of + group
She's the tallest in the class.
It's the most expensive of the three hotels.
much / a lot / far + comparative (intensifier)
This bag is much cheaper.
as + adjective + as (equality)
He's as tall as me.

6. Common errors

❌ Wrong ✅ Correct
This car is more fast. This car is faster.
She is more better. She is better.
It's the most cheapest. It's the cheapest.
Paris is more big than Rome. Paris is bigger than Rome.
He's the taller in the class. He's the tallest in the class.
