Modal "can" (ability, permission, requests)

Lesson Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Use can/can't to talk about ability, permission, and requests

  • Ask for permission politely using Can I...?
  • Make requests using Can you...?
  • Understand the pronunciation of can and can't

Input & Explanation

1. What is "can"?

Can is a modal verb that we use to talk about:

  • Ability: I can swim. She can speak Spanish.
  • Permission: Can I use your phone? You can leave early today.
  • Requests: Can you help me? Can you open the window?

2. Form: can + base verb

Can doesn't change form for different persons. We always use can + base verb (without "to").

Affirmative Negative Questions
I can swim I cannot swim / I can't swim Can I swim?
You can swim You cannot swim / You can't swim Can you swim?
He/She/It can swim He cannot swim / He can't swim Can he swim?
We can swim We cannot swim / We can't swim Can we swim?
They can swim They cannot swim / They can't swim Can they swim?
