Lesson 4: Letters & Numbers

Level: A1
Length: 60 min
Topic: Spelling, dates, phone numbers, prices
Score: 0%

Lesson Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Say and understand the English alphabet for spelling names & e-mails
  • Pronounce numbers 0-100, 1,000, 1,000,000 correctly
  • Read phone numbers, dates, postal codes, prices
  • Write an address in English layout

Spelling formula: "M-A-R-Y, Mary." Use CAPITAL letters for names, e-mails, passwords.

2. Numbers 0-20

0 – zero / oh*
1 – one /wʌn/
2 – two /tuː/
...
19 – nineteen
20 – twenty

*"oh" is common for phone numbers, room numbers.

3. Tens 20-100

20 – twenty
30 – thirty
40 – forty (drop the "u")
50 – fifty
60 – sixty
70 – seventy
80 – eighty
90 – ninety
100 – a hundred / one hundred

4. Big numbers – chunk in threes

1,000 – a / one thousand
10,000 – ten thousand
100,000 – a hundred thousand
1,000,000 – a million

UK: 1,500 = "fifteen hundred" (common)
US: always "one thousand five hundred"

5. Phone numbers – say digit by digit

020 7946 0958 → "oh-two-oh, seven-nine-four-six, oh-nine-five-eight"

Pause after 3-4 digits; use "double" & "triple":
077 766 555 → "oh-double-seven, double-six, triple-five"

6. Dates

Order: MONTH – DAY (US) vs DAY – MONTH (UK)

Spoken: "the + ordinal + of + month" (UK) or "month + ordinal" (US)
Written: 4/5 → May 4th (US) / 4 May (UK)

Ordinals 1-31:
1st first, 2nd second, 3rd third, 4th fourth… 21st twenty-first… 31st thirty-first

Year rules:
- 1999 → "nineteen ninety-nine"
- 2000 → "two thousand"
- 2008 → "two thousand and eight" / "twenty-oh-eight"
- 2025 → "twenty twenty-five"

7. Prices

£2.50 – "two pounds fifty" / "two fifty"
$1.05 – "one dollar oh-five"
€0.99 – "ninety-nine cents" (say the smallest coin)

8. E-mail & address conventions

@ → "at"
. → "dot"
_ → "underscore"
– → "dash" or "hyphen"

Example: john.smith_99@green-mail.co.uk
"john dot smith underscore ninety-nine at green-mail dot co dot uk"

Address layout (UK/US):
Name
Number + Street
Town/City – Postal code
Country

9. Common errors

❌ Wrong ✅ Correct
"I live in the street Maple." "I live on Maple Street."
"4th May, 2024" spoken as "four May" "the fourth of May twenty twenty-four"
"one hundred and fifty" ❌ US formal UK okay; US optional

Part B: Interactive Practice

1. Write the number

"twenty-three thousand, four hundred and fifty"
"fifteen hundred"
"three million, two hundred thousand"

2. Choose the correct spoken form

a) 1997
nineteen ninety-seven
nineteen and ninety-seven
b) £3.99
three pounds ninety-nine
three and ninety-nine pounds
c) 4/7 (US)
July fourth
the fourth of July

3. Correct the mistake

a) I live in Maple Street.
b) My birthday is on thirty-one of October.
c) The price is two hundreds dollars.

Part D: Fluency Mission

Record 45 seconds: spell your full name, e-mail, phone number, and say today's date in both UK & US format.

Example: "My name is Maria Rodriguez. That's M-A-R-I-A R-O-D-R-I-G-U-E-Z. My email is maria dot rodriguez at email dot com. My phone number is oh-seven-seven, double-one, double-two, triple-three. Today's date is the fifteenth of May twenty twenty-four in UK format, and May fifteenth twenty twenty-four in US format."

Tips: Speak clearly and at a moderate pace. Practice several times before recording.

Upload: private YouTube or send mp3 → teacher feedback within 48 h.

Part E: Self-Study Roadmap

Alphabet Focus

  • Practice spelling your name and common words
  • Learn the pronunciation of tricky letters (G vs J, etc.)
  • Practice spelling email addresses aloud

Numbers Practice

  • Practice numbers 0-100 until they become automatic
  • Learn the different ways to say dates in UK vs US English
  • Practice saying phone numbers and prices

Skills Practice

  • Listening: practice understanding spelled words and numbers
  • Speaking: practice giving your personal information clearly
  • Writing: practice writing addresses in English format
