Pastimes & Abilities

Level: A1-A2
Length: 60 min
Topic: Hobbies, skills, preferences
Score: 0%

Lesson Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Describe hobbies & free-time activities with accurate vocabulary
  • Use can / could for ability levels & polite requests
  • Employ be good at + noun / -ing
  • Master enjoy, love, hate, prefer + -ing
  • Invite & respond: Would you like to...? / I'd love to
  • Ask about frequency: How often...? / once/twice a week

Collocations:

go + -ing: go swimming, go shopping, go fishing

do + sport/activity: do karate, do aerobics

play + game/instrument: play volleyball, play piano

2. Ability & Level

can / can't / could (past)
I can swim 1 km. / I could swim when I was five.
be good at + noun / -ing
She is good at drawing.
be bad at / be terrible at
He is bad at remembering names.

3. Likes & Dislikes + -ing

Verb Pattern Example
enjoy + -ing I enjoy reading comics.
love + -ing They love hiking.
like + -ing / to-inf I like to jog in the morning. (both OK)
hate + -ing She hates getting up early.
prefer + -ing to -ing I prefer swimming to running.
would like + to-inf I'd like to learn Spanish. (specific wish)

NO to-inf after enjoy/love/hate: ‚úñÔ∏è I enjoy to play ‚Üí ‚úÖ I enjoy playing.

4. Frequency Adverbs & Phrases

always, usually, often, sometimes, hardly ever, never
once/twice/three times a week / month
every day / on Mondays / at weekends
How often do you...? ‚Äì Once a week.

5. Invitations & Replies

Would you like to + base...? (polite)
Would you like to play chess with me?
Replies:
Yes, I'd love to. / Sorry, I can't. I'm busy.

6. Common Errors

‚ùå Wrong ‚úÖ Correct
I enjoy to play football. I enjoy playing football.
I am good in maths. I am good at maths.
Would you like playing chess? Would you like to play chess?
I go to swim every week. I go swimming every week.
How many you do yoga? How often do you do yoga?
