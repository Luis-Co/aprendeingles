Lesson 10: Possessive 's & Possessive Pronouns

Level: A1-A2
Length: 60 min
Topic: Showing ownership with 's, s', & possessive pronouns
Score: 0%

Lesson Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Add 's or s' correctly to people & time words
  • Choose between 's and of for objects & animals
  • Use independent possessive pronouns (mine, yours, hers, ours, theirs)
  • Form questions & short answers: "Whose is this?" – "It's mine."
  • Avoid common spelling traps (its vs. it's, whose vs. who's)

Part A: Explanation & Examples

1. The apostrophe-S system

Rule Example Pronunciation
Singular noun + 's Tom's bike /tɒmz baɪk/ (add /z/ sound)
Plural ending -s + ' only the girls' room /ɡɜːlz/ (just apostrophe)
Plural NOT ending -s + 's children's toys /ˈtʃɪldrənz/ (full 's)
Names ending in -s (optional extra s) James's car OR James' car /dʒeɪmzɪz/ (both accepted)

Memory trick: Apostrophe before the s = owner is singular; after = plural owners.

2. Time & distance expressions

a ten minutes' walk
today news (NO apostrophe – adjective) vs. today's news (possessive)
a week's holiday
two years' experience

3. 's vs. "of" for things

People: Maria's ideas (natural)
Things: the door of the room (more natural than the room's door)
Animals: the dog's tail ✔️ / the tail of the dog (formal)
Countries & ships often take 's: Spain's climate, the ship's captain

4. Independent possessive pronouns

Possessive adjective (needs noun) Possessive pronoun (stands alone)
This is my pen. This pen is mine.
It's your phone. The phone is yours.
It's his camera. The camera is his.
It's her bag. The bag is hers.
It's our house. The house is ours.
It's their car. The car is theirs.

⚠️ Important: NO apostrophe in pronouns: yours not your's

5. Question word: Whose...?

Whose + noun + is this / are these?

Whose book is this? – It's John's. / It's his.
Whose are these gloves? – They're Anna's. / They're hers.

6. Double genitive (A2 stretch)

a friend of mine = one of my friends
a colleague of hers
NOT: a friend of me ❌

7. Common traps

❌ Wrong ✅ Correct
It's tail is long. Its tail is long. (possessive)
Whose coming tonight? Who's coming tonight? (who is)
The students's exam The students' exam
This book is your's. This book is yours.
