Lesson 6: Singular & Plural + Countable vs. Uncountable

Level: A1-A2
Length: 60 min
Topic: Plurals, much/many, some/any, containers
Score: 0%

Lesson Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Master irregular & foreign plurals (cactus→cacti, thesis→theses)
  • Decide quickly if a noun is countable (C) or uncountable (U)
  • Use much/many, a few/a little, some/any correctly
  • Ask "How many…?" vs. "How much…?"
  • Use containers (a bottle of, two slices of) for uncountables

Part A: Explanation & Examples

1. Tricky singular–plural spellings

Rule Singular Plural
-s / -es bus buses
consonant + y → -ies city cities
vowel + y → -s boy boys
-o + es tomato tomatoes
-o + s photo photos
-f / -fe → -ves knife knives
Same form sheep sheep
Foreign plurals cactus cacti / cactuses
Foreign plurals thesis theses

2. Countable vs. Uncountable – quick tests

Countable (C) – can pluralise: an apple – apples, a car – cars
Uncountable (U) – no plural, no "a": water, rice, information, advice, furniture, luggage, money, news, bread

Grey area:
coffee – U (liquid) / C (cups)
chicken – U (meat) / C (animals)
hair – U (all hair) / C (individual hairs)

3. Quantifiers table

With Countable nouns With Uncountable nouns
many bottles much water
a few apples a little sugar
few friends (≈ not enough) little time (≈ not enough)
some/any + plural some/any + noun
How many…? How much…?

Rule of thumb:
- many/much – questions & negatives (A1)
- a lot of – affirmative (A2)

4. Containers & partitives

a bottle of water

a cup of coffee

a slice of bread

a piece of advice

a loaf of bread

a bar of chocolate

5. Questions

How many apples do you eat a week?
How much milk do you drink?
How many bottles of water? (container + plural noun)

6. Common errors

❌ Wrong ✅ Correct
I have much friends. I have many friends.
She gave me an advice. She gave me a piece of advice.
There aren't some biscuits. There aren't any biscuits.
How much slices of bread? How many slices of bread?

Part E: Self-Study Roadmap

Grammar Focus

  • Practice forming irregular plurals
  • Learn to distinguish countable vs. uncountable nouns
  • Master the use of quantifiers (much/many, a few/a little)
  • Practice using some/any in different contexts

Vocabulary

  • Learn common container phrases
  • Study uncountable nouns that are often mistaken
  • Practice quantifier usage in different contexts

Skills Practice

  • Reading: identify countable/uncountable nouns in texts
  • Listening: notice quantifier usage in conversations
  • Speaking: practice describing quantities using correct quantifiers
  • Writing: write sentences using different quantifiers and container phrases
