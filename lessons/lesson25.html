At the Restaurant

Level: A1-A2
Length: 60 min
Topic: Ordering food, dealing with problems
Score: 0%

Lesson Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Book a table on the phone & check details
  • Order politely: starters, mains, sides, desserts, drinks
  • Ask for the bill & split it
  • Handle common problems (wrong dish, cold food, allergies)
  • Use "would like", "could I...?", intonation for courtesy

Input & Explanation

1. Booking by Phone

I'd like to book a table for four at 8 p.m., please.
Could we have a table by the window?
Is there any availability for tomorrow night?
May I have your name? – Sure, it's García.

2. Arriving & Being Seated

We have a reservation under García.
Could we sit outside?
Would you like still or sparkling water? (UK house custom)

3. Menu Sections

Starters / Appetizers

Soup of the day £5.50
Garlic bread £4.00

Main Courses

Grilled salmon £16.50
Vegetable pasta £12.00

4. Ordering Politely

Waiter Phrases

Are you ready to order?

Would you like any starters?

How would you like your steak? (rare, medium, well-done)

Customer Phrases

I'll have the grilled salmon, please.

Could I have the soup to start?

No starters for me, thank you.

Can we share a dessert?

5. During the Meal

Is everything all right? – Yes, delicious / lovely, thanks.
Could I have some ketchup, please?
Would you like another drink? – Yes, please. / I'm fine, thanks.

6. Problems & Complaints (Polite)

Excuse me, this isn't what I ordered.
I'm afraid my soup is cold.
Could you bring me the right dish?
I asked for no nuts—sorry, I'm allergic.
No problem at all, I'll change it straight away. (waiter)

7. Bill / Check

Could we have the bill, please? (UK)
Can we pay separately? / Is it possible to split the bill?
I'll pay by card. / Do you take contactless?
Service is included / not included.

8. Common Errors

❌ Wrong ✅ Correct
I want a soup. I'd like some soup. / I'll have the soup.
Can you bring me the account? Can you bring me the bill / check?
We pay separate. We pay separately. / Can we split the bill?
I ordered for chicken. I ordered chicken. (no preposition)
