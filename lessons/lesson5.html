Lesson 5: Nouns & Pronouns

Level: A1
Length: 60 min
Topic: Singular/plural nouns, personal & possessive pronouns
Score: 0%

Lesson Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Recognise countable vs. uncountable nouns
  • Form regular & common irregular plurals
  • Replace nouns with correct personal pronouns (I, you, he, she, it, we, they)
  • Use possessive adjectives & possessive pronouns accurately (my/mine, your/yours...)

Part A: Explanation & Examples

1. Countable vs. uncountable

Countable (C) Uncountable (U)
have a plural no plural
a/an + singular some + noun
chair→chairs f furniture ✖️furnitures
apple→apples advice ✖️advices

Quick test: Can you put "a" or "two" before it?
✅ a book / two books → C
✖️a water / two waters → usually U (except "two waters" in restaurants)

2. Regular plural rules

  1. add -s cat→cats
  2. -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -z → -es bus→buses, dish→dishes
  3. consonant + y → -ies baby→babies
  4. vowel + y → -s boy→boys
  5. -o endings:
    +es hero→heroes, tomato→tomatoes
    +s photo→photos, piano→pianos (check dictionary)
  6. -f / -fe → -ves knife→knives, leaf→leaves (but roof→roofs)

3. Common irregular plurals

man→men
woman→women /ˈwɪmɪn/
child→children
foot→feet
tooth→teeth
mouse→mice
goose→geese
person→people (also "persons" in formal signs)
sheep→sheep (same)
fish→fish (fishes = different species, A2+)

4. Personal pronouns (subject)

Person Singular Plural
1st I we
2nd you you
3rd he/she/it they

Object pronouns (quick peek): me, you, him, her, it, us, them – we practise mainly subject today.

5. Possessive adjectives vs possessive pronouns

Possessive adjective + noun Possessive pronoun (alone)
This is my book. The book is mine.
That is your car. The car is yours.
His / her / its his / hers / its (no apostrophe!)
This is our house. The house is ours.
That is their dog. The dog is theirs.

⚠️ its (possessive) ≠ it's (it is / it has)

6. Quick decision flow

  1. Need a subject? → personal pronoun I, you, he...
  2. Need to show ownership before a noun? → possessive adjective my, your...
  3. Need to show ownership without repeating the noun? → possessive pronoun mine, yours...

7. Common errors

❌ Wrong ✅ Correct
He is my father. His name is Tom. ✔️
The books are her. The books are hers.
Its raining. It's raining.
Two womans Two women
Many peoples say... Many people say... (people = plural)

Part E: Self-Study Roadmap

Grammar Focus

  • Practice forming regular and irregular plurals
  • Learn to distinguish countable vs. uncountable nouns
  • Master the use of personal pronouns
  • Practice possessive adjectives vs. possessive pronouns

Vocabulary

  • Learn common irregular plural nouns
  • Study uncountable nouns that are often mistaken
  • Practice pronoun usage in different contexts

Skills Practice

  • Reading: identify nouns and pronouns in texts
  • Listening: notice pronoun usage in conversations
  • Speaking: practice describing objects and people using correct pronouns
  • Writing: write sentences using different types of nouns and pronouns
